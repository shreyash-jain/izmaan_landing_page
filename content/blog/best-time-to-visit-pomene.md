"When should we come?" is the question we're asked most — and the honest answer is that **there's no bad time to visit Pomene.** The water is warm all year, the beaches are always empty, and the reserve simply hands you something different each season.

This is a month-by-month guide to help you time your trip around what matters most to you: whales, turtles, fishing, diving, kitesurfing — or simply the most comfortable weather.

## Key takeaways

- **Warm water year-round** — Pomene sits in the warm Mozambique Channel.
- **Whales:** roughly **July–November** (peak Sept–Oct).
- **Turtles:** nesting **October–March**.
- **Fishing, diving & kitesurfing:** good **all year**.
- **Most comfortable weather:** the cooler, drier months, roughly **May–September**.

## Pomene weather at a glance

Pomene has a warm tropical climate with two loose seasons:

- **Cooler & drier (≈ May–September):** bright days, comfortable temperatures, easy travel. The most popular window for first-timers.
- **Warmer & wetter (≈ November–March):** hotter and more humid, with rain usually arriving in short bursts rather than washout days. This is prime **turtle** season.

Sea temperatures stay swim-friendly throughout, which is why diving and snorkelling never really go out of season here.

## The wildlife calendar

### Humpback whales — July to November

From around **July to November**, Pomene sits right on the humpback migration path as the whales move north to breed in the warm channel. Because the continental shelf runs close to shore, they often pass **near enough to watch from the dune** — and closer still from a boat.

- **July–August:** the migration builds; first reliable sightings.
- **September–October:** peak activity, including breaching and tail-slapping.
- **November:** mothers and calves on the return leg.

Early, glassy mornings give the clearest sightings. Bring binoculars and a little patience.

### Nesting turtles — October to March

From about **October to March**, the beaches become a nursery. **Loggerhead and leatherback** turtles haul ashore after dark to lay their eggs, and — weeks later — hatchlings make their dash to the sea.

Watching is a privilege that comes with rules:

- **No white light** — use a red-filtered torch only, kept low.
- **Keep your distance** and stay still.
- **Never touch** turtles or hatchlings, and fill in any holes dug on the beach.

An off-grid, dark-sky beach like Pomene's is exactly what hatchlings need: they navigate to the ocean by the natural glow of the horizon, and artificial light confuses them.

## The activity calendar

### Fishing — year-round
Pomene's reputation was built on its **reef and game fishing**, and it holds up all year. It's the reason many families first discovered the area decades ago.

### Diving & snorkelling — year-round
Warm water and a reef close to shore make for excellent, uncrowded diving and snorkelling in every season. Visibility is generally best on calmer days.

### Kitesurfing — year-round
Steady cross-shore wind over a wide, empty lagoon gives consistent, uncrowded conditions whenever you visit.

## So, when should *you* go?

- **Want whales?** Aim for **September–October**.
- **Want turtles?** Come **November–February**.
- **Want the easiest weather?** Choose **May–September**.
- **Want fishing, diving or kite?** Any month works — book around your own calendar.

Whichever you choose, you'll find the same warm water and the same empty beach.
